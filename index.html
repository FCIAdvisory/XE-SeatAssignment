Why it matters
The real risk

It's not the move. It's the plan behind it.

A move or a growth spurt touches every employee and locks in how teams work for years.

It's tempting to treat it as a facilities project. But the costliest failures almost never trace to the physical logistics — they trace to the planning. Get the foundation right and everything downstream, cost, schedule, and adoption, gets easier.

Where moves go wrong
Requirements no one validated before space was drawn
Stakeholders aligned too late — or not at all
Seats decided by grade, or by who asks loudest
A plan that's obsolete within a year of growth
No governance — so every future change is a negotiation

A weak foundation is the most expensive thing in any move — and the least visible until it's too late to fix.

Our method
The signature method · the Seat-Fit Index

Turn seating from a negotiation into a standard you own.

Score every role against objective, weighted criteria you set. The score sets the space type (by need, not grade) and the placement (by who they work with) — defensible, equitable, and repeatable.
